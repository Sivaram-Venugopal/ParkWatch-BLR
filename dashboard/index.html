Methodology notes — read before judging the numbers

Three honest caveats about what this data can and cannot show.

No ground-truth congestion data
The raw dataset contains violation records only — no road speed, flow, or volume measurements. The Congestion Impact Score (CIS) is a transparent proxy built from violation severity, vehicle footprint, and time-of-day traffic weight, not a measured delay.
Enforcement is night-skewed
~75% of detections occur between 9pm-8am, reflecting when enforcement devices are active, not when illegal parking peaks. The time-of-day weight in CIS partially corrects for this, but daytime parking impact is likely undercounted in raw volume.
Grid zoning, not density clustering
DBSCAN clustering was tested and rejected — Bengaluru's dense old-city core chained into one mega-cluster covering 55% of all violations. Fixed 150m grid cells were used instead for stable, patrol-beat-sized zones.
ParkWatch BLR — prototype dashboard for Tata Technologies InnoVent submission. Data: Jan–May 2024 anonymized police violation records, Bengaluru Traffic Police. All zone boundaries and scores are derived; see solution documentation for full methodology.
